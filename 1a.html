Grammar Reference
1. Present Simple vs Present Continuous ▼

Present Simple: V / V-s/es

  • Habits / Routines: People in Japan bow when they meet.
  • Schedules: The train leaves at 8:30 a.m.
  • Facts / cultural truths: In Thailand, showing your feet is rude.

Present Continuous: am/is/are + V-ing

  • Actions happening now: They are discussing body language.
  • Fixed future arrangements: We’re having a cultural fair this Sunday.
  • Temporary actions: I’m learning about Chinese gestures.
  • Annoying habits (with “always”): He’s always interrupting others.
Examples:
- She usually bows when she meets someone.
- Look! They are taking photos.
2. Stative vs Dynamic Verbs ▼

Stative verbs describe states, feelings, thoughts, possession – usually not used in continuous forms.

Dynamic verbs describe actions – can be used in continuous forms.

Verb Stative meaning (no continuous) Dynamic meaning (can be continuous)
think có ý kiến: I think she is right. cân nhắc, suy nghĩ: I am thinking about my future.
have sở hữu: She has a new bike. dùng trong hoạt động: We are having dinner now.
see hiểu: I see what you mean. gặp gỡ: I am seeing the dentist this afternoon.
appear có vẻ như: He appears tired. biểu diễn / tham gia: She is appearing in a play tonight.
taste có vị: The soup tastes salty. nếm thử: He is tasting the sauce.
feel có cảm xúc / ý kiến: I feel happy today. cảm nhận thể chất: She is feeling the fabric.
