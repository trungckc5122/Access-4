Reported Speech (Indirect Speech) ?

Form: Changes to verb tenses, pronouns, and time/place expressions.

Key rules (mandatory backshift):

  • Present Simple ? Past Simple: "I like it." ? He said he liked it.
  • Present Progressive ? Past Progressive: "I am studying." ? She said she was studying.
  • Present Perfect ? Past Perfect: "I have finished." ? He said he had finished.
  • Past Simple ? Past Perfect: "I saw it." ? She said she had seen it.
  • Will ? Would: "I will help." ? He said he would help.

Pronoun Changes in Reported Speech:

you, your, yoursI/we, my/our, mine/ours (or he/she, his/her)

From Ashes to Discoveries: The Language of Fossils

Scientists often embark on an expedition to find dinosaur remains. In places like the Gobi Desert, volcanic ashes covered the ground and helped preserve bones. Over time, wind and rain scatter the material, but careful work allows researchers to retrace ancient paths. Each fossil helps unfold the story of life on Earth. These discoveries prove that mammals lived alongside dinosaurs, and they reveal feeding habits of creatures long gone. Such sites become a true dinosaur graveyard, offering a glimpse into a lost world.
