Why music is important to me

by Amy Walsh, aged 16

I've always been creative and by the time I started secondary school, I was already doing loads of music in my free time. In my first year, because I particularly loved singing, I decided to set up a band, and asked some school friends to join. We learnt songs by popular girl bands, and performed them at school events. And for one special concert, we even sold tickets with our signatures on them – in case we ever became famous one day!

But then, in my second year, I gradually started giving up some of my musical activities. I'd like to say it was because my schoolwork became more challenging, but I think it was actually more about me developing different interests. I preferred spending my free time going shopping with classmates, or playing tennis – which to my surprise, I discovered I had a real talent for.

However, after a while, I started missing all the music – especially singing. Joining a singing group seemed like the answer. The one at school already had too many members, though, so I found a small singing group in my town instead. But I had to audition – in other words, sing solo in front of some group members and let them decide whether I was good enough. It was fair, I guess, but I was really nervous. Somehow, though, my performance satisfied them, and I was allowed to join. When they told me, I couldn't stop smiling!

That was two years ago – and I still love singing with the group. I've met some great people, and our shows are amazing, too. I especially love the huge excitement of being in a really great show, with the audience all clapping. There's really nothing like it! The only thing is that we practise on Thursday evenings – that doesn't always work well with my schoolwork, so I sometimes miss it.

Singing in the group has also made me want to write my own songs. I found it hard to write good words to go with my music, though. Then I remembered a teacher saying that music takes you to places that words simply can't reach. So maybe it's OK just to focus on the music!
