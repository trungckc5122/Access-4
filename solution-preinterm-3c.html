3C Listening

I can listen for gist and details.

💡 Listening Strategy

Trong các bài tập nghe, phần hướng dẫn và câu hỏi thường cho bạn một vài ý tưởng về loại chủ đề và loại ngôn ngữ mà bạn sắp nghe. Đọc kỹ chúng trước khi nghe sẽ giúp bạn 'bắt nhịp' tốt hơn.

Transcript 1.10

1.
Man: Are you hungry? I could make some lunch.
Woman: Starving. What have we got?
Man: Er … we’ve got some bananas. And a packet of rice. And a tomato. Ooh – and some cheese, but it looks a bit old. How does that sound? Hey! Where are you going?
Woman: Back soon!
Narrator: When you’re hungry – and you don’t fancy rice with bananas and cheese – there’s always another option.
Woman: Two portions of chicken with fries, please. And a side order of garlic bread.
Server: Certainly, madam. To eat here?
Woman: No, take away.
Narrator: The Chicken Shack – for delicious American-style chicken. There’s one near you.
2.
Colours that match your natural beauty. More than a hundred different shades. A style to match your outfit, whatever you’re wearing. Elegant and stylish. Natural products for eyes, lips and face. Just ask for Pour Elle – and you’re looking beautiful.
3.
Narrator: What matters in your life?
Man: Being fit and healthy.
Woman: Feeling good.
Boy: Looking cool!
Narrator: Whatever matters, make East Coast fashion a part of your life, whether you’re playing football with friends, running a race or just chilling. Life is a game. Play it well. Wear East Coast casuals. In department stores now.
4.
Man: Hi Sarah. Can I give you a lift?
Woman: Yes, thanks. Oh … is this new?
Man: Yes. I got it yesterday!
Woman: Nice!
Man: It’s great to drive. I love it.
Woman: Yes, really smooth and comfortable.
Man: It’s a two litre engine. Six cylinders.
Woman: Can I ask you something?
Man: Yes, sure.
Woman: Can I have a go at driving it?
Man: Sure. Why not?
Narrator: The new XK 44 – on the road from £19,995.

Transcript 1.11

1.
Man: There’s a great film on tonight at the Picture House.
Woman: Really? What kind of film?
Man: It’s a comedy.
Woman: I’m really tired. I think I need an evening at home.
Man: Oh, come on. I don’t want to go by myself.
Woman: Well, ask a friend.
Man: But I want to go with you! We hardly ever go out. I really think you should make more effort!
Woman: I’m sorry – but I’m always so tired. I work hard, you know!
2.
Boy: Did I tell you what happened to me when I was out with Molly? ... Well, we were at the cinema. The film was starting when Molly stood up and whispered something about going to buy popcorn. So she went... a few moments later, she came back and sat down... I helped myself to some of the popcorn. She was holding a big box of it in her hand. And a woman’s voice said, ‘Hey! That’s mine!’... it wasn’t Molly! It was another woman who was sitting in her place.
3.
Boy: What’s on next?
Girl: There’s a talent show starting in five minutes.
Boy: No thanks! I really don’t like talent shows.
Girl: Me neither. They’re so boring, aren’t they?
Boy: Yes. Terrible. What else is on?
Girl: There’s a new sitcom on a bit later.
Boy: No, sitcoms aren’t my thing. They’re never really funny.
Girl: I don't think you're right about sitcoms. Some of the American shows are really clever.
4.
Woman: Can I help you?
Man: Yes, I want to return this DVD.
Woman: Have you watched it?
Man: Yes, I have.
Woman: Well, I’m sorry. You can’t return it.
Man: But there’s a problem with it. It keeps jumping. And some of the scenes don’t play at all.
Woman: It's just not good enough... Where’s the manager?
