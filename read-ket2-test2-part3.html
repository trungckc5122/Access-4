"I first became a tour guide by accident, really. My aunt worked for a tour company and asked me if I'd like to do some guiding in my university holidays. I was studying history, which is useful, but you don't need a degree to become a tour guide. I'd never thought about doing it before, but I enjoyed it so much that I decided to do it full-time when I finished my course."

"The job isn't easy. I have to prepare for each tour carefully. Sometimes I'm at an airport collecting people who don't know where to go, and sometimes I take tourists to places. I must always have a list of who's on the tour, and I check I've got the maps I need before I start."

"I do the same thing day after day, but I love it. I'm not someone who likes sitting in an office, and there are lots of chances to meet new people. The best thing is seeing tourists having a really good holiday. They go home happy, and that makes me feel good."

"I work with other guides, and we're all good friends. If any of us are in another city with a tour group, we let the other guides know about good places for lunch, or maybe something special that tourists might want to see."

"If you're thinking of becoming a tour guide, I'd say go on a tour first. Get a feel for what's involved. I don't think you can really understand what it's like until you've actually done it. But it's the best job in the world!"
