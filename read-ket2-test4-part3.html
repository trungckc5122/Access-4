How schoolboy Jonathan Davis found some very old silver.

"When I'm not studying for school, I enjoy metal detecting," says 15-year-old Jonathan Davis. "I have a lot of hobbies, but this is my favourite. I bought my detector with money I got for my birthday. I don't live near the sea, so I can't do beach detecting like many people, but I'm happy looking for interesting things in fields near my village, Saxby."

"One cold Saturday afternoon, my detector started making a noise, so I started digging. The soil was very hard because of the bad weather we'd been having. I couldn't dig very deep, but I soon saw something. I thought it was just a piece of metal, so I wasn't very excited. But I kept on digging and I found seven more pieces. They were all about 40 cm down in the ground."

"I phoned my friend Josh. He's interested in archaeology too, and I thought he'd want to see them. I was surprised by how much he liked them. He said they were really special and I should take them to the museum. When I did that, the staff were very interested. They said they'd never seen anything like them before. I felt really pleased."

"The staff told me the metal was 2,000 years old and from another country. They explained that in the past people made the silver into a shape like a plate and used it to buy and sell things. I was surprised that the silver had been in the ground for so long. The staff were pleased because they'll be able to show people that part of the history of my village."

"My silver is now in the museum. I visit it whenever I can, and I really like seeing other people looking at it. Josh and I have also been to lots of schools to talk about metal detecting and what we found. I hope lots of other teenagers will take up this hobby! I still can't believe I found something so amazing."
