READING PASSAGE · Part 4
Writing for the school newspaper

Writing articles for your school newspaper can be fun and interesting. You can report on everything from a change in school rules to the results of the school team's basketball match. There are all kinds of stories waiting to be told. [ 16 ] ✕ In fact, the two styles are quite different.

One difference is that, when a journalist is writing their news story, they include all of the basic facts in the first paragraph. Later paragraphs contain the rest of the information in order, from most to least important. [ 17 ] ✕ It is sometimes necessary to do this because, when the newspaper is put together, there isn't enough space for everything. Deleting the last few paragraphs of a story is the easiest way to fit it all in.

A journalist's responsibility is to report news accurately. [ 18 ] ✕ These may include sports scores, the spelling of names and the location of events. Write them down in a notepad, and if you can, record interviews. This will help to ensure that your finished article doesn't contain mistakes.

News stories must deliver the facts quickly, but as well as informing readers, you need to make your story interesting. [ 19 ] ✕ For example, write about what the crowd at an event thought about it. At a sports event, talk to the team captain and include their words in your report. And take plenty of photos to accompany your article.

If your story is about something that different people have different feelings about, make sure that you include everyone's opinions. [ 20 ] ✕ Instead, you have to let readers make up their own minds by reporting both sides of the argument.

When your article is finished, give it a final spelling and grammar check, and add a headline. And don't forget your name. You want everyone to know who wrote that amazing article!
