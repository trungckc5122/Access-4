READING PASSAGE · EIGHT FANTASTIC TOURIST RAILWAYS A–H
A. Hubberton Railway

This small railway follows a beautiful route through different types of countryside. There's a café on the train serving delicious snacks and drinks. Fun quizzes and games are organised for kids and teenagers at the stations, one of which also has a small museum.

B. Kerry Valley Railway

Although the museum at Kerry station closed last year, this railway still provides a great day out. Volunteers on board will inform you about interesting things to look out for during the train journey, and there are treasure hunts and music at each stop on the route. There's a special offer on tickets for parents with children.

C. Amberton Railway

Perfect for a special day out, this railway line's fun for everyone, with cheap family tickets and great-value sandwiches and cakes available on the train and at stations. There's lots to see from the train, including birds and other animals, if you look carefully. There's also a lovely hotel next to Amberton station.

D. Southland Railway

Southland station has a great museum with photographs and objects from when this seaside railway was first built right up to the present day. Don't miss the fantastic film shown there, explaining what the Southland Railway staff do every day at work. There's also reasonably priced accommodation within a few minutes' walk.

E. Cramford Railway

This route goes through countryside full of birds and wild animals. Before you set off, you'll get a list of questions about what you'll see from the train. Answer them all correctly during the journey, and you'll win a great souvenir of your trip! Staff can even organise cakes and food on board for a special occasion.

F. Edgeley Railway

This route was made famous in the movie The Line, as you'll certainly hear from the guide on your train. You can even hire the whole train and get the chance to sit next to the driver, for that really special birthday celebration. Interesting souvenirs are available at each station.

G. Viaduct Railway

During your journey, the scenery will keep on changing – you'll never get bored! There are stalls at each of the stations – you'll definitely find something to remember your visit by. Famous for its wonderful restaurant, the Viaduct Hotel at the end of this lovely route is a great place to stay.

H. Delland Railway

This railway runs just a few metres from the sea between Delland and Tarford, so watch carefully for dolphins! You'll possibly recognise the railway from movies, as it's appeared in several popular ones. Low-cost tickets are available for parents with children, and you'll have the chance to buy snacks, drinks and light meals during your journey.
