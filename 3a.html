Grammar Reference & Study Notes

Present Perfect vs. Past Simple:

  • Past Simple: Used for completed actions at a specific time in the past ("I visited Kyoto last summer")
  • Present Perfect: Used for actions that happened at an unspecified time or have a connection to the present ("I have finished my homework already")
  • Signal words: Past Simple: yesterday, last week, ago, in 2022. Present Perfect: already, yet, never, ever, since, for, just
  • Note: Present Perfect often focuses on the result or experience, not when it happened

Relative Clauses:

  • Who: Used for people ("the mother who prepared...")
  • Which: Used for things and animals ("the magazine which provides...")
  • That: Used for both people and things in defining relative clauses ("the ingredients that are used...")
  • Where: Used for places ("the kitchen where we cook...")
  • Whose: Used to show possession ("the boy whose favorite food...")
  • Note: In defining relative clauses, 'that' can replace 'who' or 'which'

Passive Voice:

  • Structure: Subject + to be + past participle + (by agent)
  • Present Simple: am/is/are + past participle ("Obento is prepared")
  • Past Simple: was/were + past participle ("A new menu was introduced")
  • Present Perfect: have/has been + past participle ("Snacks have been removed")
  • Future: will be + past participle ("A workshop will be organized")

Grammar references based on Cambridge English Grammar in Use and Oxford English Grammar Course.
