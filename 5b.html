Grammar Reference & Study Notes

Indefinite Pronouns:

  • some-: Used in positive sentences ("someone", "something", "somewhere")
  • any-: Used in questions and negative sentences ("anyone", "anything", "anywhere")
  • no-: Used to express absence ("nobody", "nothing", "nowhere")
  • every-: Used to refer to all ("everyone", "everything", "everywhere")
  • Usage:
    • somebody = a person (positive sentences)
    • anybody = any person (questions/negatives)
    • nobody = no person (negative meaning)
    • everybody = all people

Quantifiers: a lot of / many / much

  • many: Used with countable nouns (many birds, many students)
  • much: Used with uncountable nouns (much information, much time)
  • a lot of: Can be used with both countable and uncountable nouns
  • Negative forms:
    • not many (countable)
    • not much (uncountable)
  • Questions:
    • How many...? (countable)
    • How much...? (uncountable)

Animal Senses Vocabulary:

  • Senses: The five ways humans and animals understand the world (sight, hearing, touch, taste, smell)
  • Vision: The ability to see things clearly
  • Capability: The skill or power to do something
  • Qualify: To be considered as something (often with "as")
  • Note: Different animals have different sensory capabilities adapted to their environments

Grammar references based on Cambridge English Grammar in Use and Oxford English Grammar Course.
