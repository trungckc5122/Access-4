Writing an Informal Letter: Congratulating a Friend
1. Purpose (Mục đích)

Viết thư mừng bạn bè khi họ đạt được một thành tích, giải thưởng, học bổng hay cơ hội đặc biệt (như cuộc thi, đội thể thao, v.v.).

2. Structure (Bố cục)
PartNội dung chính
GreetingChào hỏi thân mật (Hi/Hello + tên, Dear + tên)
OpeningNêu lý do viết thư (vừa biết tin vui)
BodyChúc mừng – tại sao xứng đáng – cơ hội từ giải thưởng này
ClosingLời chúc thêm / mong được hồi âm
Sign-offLời kết thân mật (Best wishes, Love, Take care...)
3. Useful Sentence Structures

Opening

  • I just heard the great news!
  • I'm so happy to know that...
  • It was wonderful to hear about your...

Congratulating & Expressing pride

  • Congratulations on your achievement/success!
  • You really deserve it after all your effort.
  • I'm so proud of you!
  • Well done! All that hard work really paid off.

Expressing feelings & Support

  • I'm sure you'll do amazing there.
  • You must be very excited about this chance.
  • I can't wait to hear about your experience.
  • This is such an exciting opportunity for you.

Closing

  • Write back soon!
  • Hope everything goes well.
  • Looking forward to hearing all about it.
  • Take care and enjoy every moment.
4. Example Letter

Hi Jack,

I just heard the great news about your scholarship! Congratulations – you really deserve it after all your hard work. I'm so proud of you and I'm sure you'll have an amazing time there.

It must be such an exciting opportunity to learn new things and meet new people. Write back and tell me all about it when you get there!

Best wishes,
Trung

6. Quick Reference: Informal Letter Style
DoDon't
Use contractions (I'm, you'll, can't, don't)Use overly formal language (I would like to inform you...)
Start with a friendly greeting (Hi/Hello + name)Start with a very stiff opening (Dear Sir or Madam)
Share your feelings and excitementWrite only facts without any personal touch
Use exclamation marks occasionally for warmthForget to sign off with a friendly closing
Keep the letter short and personalMake it too long or include unnecessary details
✅ Remember: an informal letter is like a conversation with a friend – be natural, warm, and supportive.
