READING PASSAGE · Part 3
Young people's cooking school

by Tom Alvarez, aged 13

When I was three, my mum and I started making biscuits together. We used cookie cutters shaped like cartoon monsters, which I loved. It was always like a game and never too challenging. I'm so grateful to my mum for introducing me to cooking that way. I think that's the main reason why I love it so much now.

I sometimes watch TV programmes about cooking, but they're about so many different kinds of things that it can be really hard to choose which one to watch. I sometimes wonder where the chefs find their unusual ingredients, and a lot of the time their methods are so difficult that I lose interest. There are some programmes about making dishes that are good for you, but I'm not sure how many teenagers actually watch those.

Because I'm so keen on cooking, last month my parents paid for me to go on a one-day cookery course, especially designed for 10–14-year-olds, at a famous restaurant. We all went to the restaurant kitchen and our teacher, Maurice, gave us white aprons and hats: it was just like being a proper chef! The others were all younger than me – I hoped they wouldn't expect me to be the best. Before we started, we had juice and snacks while we decided what we wanted to make: desserts or pasta dishes. I chose a pasta dish.

After that, we didn't sit down again for two hours – there was loads to do. We watched Maurice carefully show us exactly what we had to do at each stage, then we had a go ourselves. One boy dropped his ingredients all over the floor, but Maurice just smiled and showed us how to arrange our plates, pans and stuff to make the best use of our space – that's advice I'll never forget. I left my pasta to boil for five extra minutes, and I didn't put quite enough salt in the sauce, but it was still pretty good. When we'd finished, we all sat down in the main restaurant and enjoyed tasting what everyone had produced. If I ever get to do another course there, I think I'll pick desserts, because those were amazing.
