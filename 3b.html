Grammar Reference & Study Notes

Present Perfect Tense:

  • Form: have/has + past participle (e.g., "have seen", "has eaten")
  • Usage:
    • Actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past
    • Experiences in someone's life
    • Actions that started in the past and continue to the present
    • Recent actions with present relevance
  • Signal words: already, yet, just, ever, never, for, since, recently

Present Perfect Continuous Tense:

  • Form: have/has + been + present participle (-ing form)
  • Usage:
    • Actions that started in the past and are still continuing
    • Actions that have just stopped and have visible results
    • Emphasizing the duration of an action
  • Signal words: for, since, all day, how long, recently

Key Differences:

  • Present Perfect: Focuses on the result or completion of the action
  • Present Perfect Continuous: Focuses on the duration or process of the action
  • Examples:
    • "I have read 50 pages." (Result: I finished 50 pages)
    • "I have been reading for 2 hours." (Duration: 2 hours of reading)
