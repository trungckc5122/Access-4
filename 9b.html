Vocabulary Notes ▼

Sự khác biệt trong cách dùng:

  • dismal - nhấn mạnh không khí buồn, u ảm, trống trải
    Thường dùng cho place, situation, experience
    Ví dụ: a dismal street, a dismal experience
  • dreadful - nhấn mạnh cảm giác sợ hãi, kinh hoàng khi nhìn hoặc trải qua
    Thường dùng cho sight, feeling, event
    Ví dụ: a dreadful sight, dreadful fear
  • calamitous - nhấn mạnh mức độ nghiêm trọng, hậu quả lớn, mang tính thảm họa
    Thường dùng cho disaster, event
    Ví dụ: a calamitous disaster
Grammar Notes: Introductory Verbs ▼

Introductory Verbs in Reported Speech:

Different introductory verbs require different grammatical structures:

  • promise/offer/agree/threaten + to + infinitive
    Example: "I'll help you." → He offered to help me.
  • remind/warn/advise/beg + object + to + infinitive
    Example: "Lock the door." → She reminded me to lock the door.
  • complain/announce/insist + that + clause
    Example: "He's always lying." → She complained that he was always lying.
  • apologize for + gerund
    Example: "I'm sorry I was rude." → He apologized for being rude.
  • insist on + gerund
    Example: "I'm going to help." → He insisted on helping.

Note: Verb tenses usually change in reported speech (present → past, will → would, etc.).

Phrasal Verbs Notes: "break" ▼

Common Phrasal Verbs with "break":

  • break out - to start suddenly (usually something dangerous or unpleasant)
    Example: A fire broke out in the building.
  • break up - to end a romantic relationship
    Example: They broke up after three years together.
  • break through - to achieve success after overcoming obstacles
    Example: She finally broke through as an actress.
  • break into - to enter by force (usually illegally)
    Example: Thieves broke into the house last night.
  • break off - to stop doing something suddenly
    Example: They broke off negotiations.
  • break down - 1) to stop working (machines), 2) to fail (plans), 3) to become very upset (people)
    Example: The car broke down on the highway.

The Great Fire of London – A Calamitous Event

The plague of 1665 had barely ended when London faced another disaster: a fire that broke out in a bakery. John Evelyn’s account gives us a vivid picture of the dismal and dreadful scene. People were so distracted by fear that they could only run, not even trying to salvage their belongings. The flames continued to rage for days, turning the city into a ruin. It was a truly calamitous event. Yet from the ashes, Londoners showed resilience, finding shelter and eventually rebuilding. The fire ultimately slowed down only after blowing up houses to create firebreaks. This historical event reminds us how disasters can break down a city but also how courage can break through despair.

Đại dịch hạch năm 1665 chưa kịp lắng xuống thì London lại phải đối mặt với một thảm họa khác: một đám cháy bùng phát từ tiệm bánh. Lời kể của John Evelyn vẽ nên bức tranh u ám và khủng khiếp. Sự kiện lịch sử này nhắc nhở chúng ta rằng thảm họa có thể phá hủy một thành phố, nhưng lòng dũng cảm có thể vượt qua tuyệt vọng.
