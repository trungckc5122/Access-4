READING PASSAGE · Part 3
Swimmer Sally Phillips

Sports reporter Karen Wood visited swimmer Sally Phillips, and discovered a new approach to swimming competitions.

Sally Phillips is an open-water swimmer – a swimmer who competes in outdoor competitions rather than indoor swimming pools. Sally is going to compete in the next international open-water swimming competition, which takes place at the harbour in Barcelona, Spain. Thousands of people are expected to come and watch the competition from the beach. 'I can't wait,' she says. 'I'm really looking forward to getting in the water. When I get nervous before a competition, just thinking about the joy of swimming makes me feel much better.'

Sally wasn't always a swimmer though – at school she was more interested in long-distance running. She'd given it up when she had a leg injury during a race, and then she tried cycling for a while. 'I enjoyed it, but I didn't find cycling very exciting,' she recalls. 'Then I tried swimming, and suddenly everything made sense. I realised that the swimming pool wasn't for me, and as soon as I started swimming in open water, I was hooked!'

However, during an open-water race, swimmers have to watch out for potential dangers in the sea, and that can be hard work. 'My parents were worried about me at first because the sea's deep and cold, and the waves can be big. I need someone following me in a boat, keeping an eye on me and making sure I don't get lost,' Sally says. 'It can be challenging, but that makes crossing the finishing line even better!'

Sally has been in several regional competitions and has won a couple of prizes. However, she isn't interested in setting records, or being the first to finish. 'What I love is the journey – taking the time to look at the sky, the beach and the sea life around me. When I'm in the water, I just enjoy the moment.'
