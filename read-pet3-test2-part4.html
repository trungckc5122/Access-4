The kakapo: saving an unusual bird

The kakapo, a type of parrot, is a New Zealand bird. Kakapos are unusual for two reasons. Firstly, they weigh a lot – up to 5 kg in some cases. Secondly, although they were once found in large numbers throughout the country, they have come close to disappearing, partly because they have, over time, lost the ability to fly. [ 16 ] ✕ But that is not the only reason. Another issue is that kakapos typically only produce babies when a particular tree, the rimu tree, has a large amount of fruit available for them to eat. [ 17 ] ✕ As a result, there are sometimes long periods of time during which no new babies are born, which affects overall numbers.

One year, when researchers were doing a project on the birds, they were only able to find 51 of them. The New Zealand government realised that something needed to be done, so they decided to change the birds' location. [ 18 ] ✕ None of the animals that typically hunt them lived there. When the kakapos arrived, they were fitted with special devices which have allowed researchers to study the birds ever since, and see where they are on the islands. [ 19 ] ✕ The technology gives the birds access to food, by electronically opening or closing food boxes.

When a female kakapo produces a lot of eggs, researchers sometimes remove some. This is done when, for example, the mother bird would find it hard to feed a larger number of babies. The eggs are kept in a warm room until the young kakapos are born. The birds are then cared for by the researchers for four months, at which point they are put back out on the island. [ 20 ] ✕ Then, of course, there would be a negative effect on future numbers. Fortunately, the project seems to be working, as the population of kakapos is increasing.
