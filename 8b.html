Grammar Reference & Study Notes

Modal Verbs for Deduction (must, can't, may/might):

  • Must: strong evidence ("The lights are off. They must be out.")
  • Can't: strong negative evidence ("He can't be at home. His car is gone.")
  • May/Might: uncertain ("She might be in a meeting.")
  • Note: "Can't" is the opposite of "must" for negative deductions

Causative Form (have/get something done):

TenseStructureExample
Present Simplehave/has + object + past participleI have my car serviced every year.
Past Simplehad + object + past participleShe had her hair cut yesterday.
Present Continuousam/is/are having + object + past participleWe are having our house painted.
Futurewill have + object + past participleThey will have the roof repaired next week.
Present Perfecthave/has had + object + past participleHe has had his eyes tested recently.
