Grammar Reference

Relative Clauses

Relative clauses give more information about a noun. They are introduced by relative pronouns:

  • who - for people (subject)
  • whom - for people (object, formal)
  • which - for things
  • that - for people or things
  • whose - for possession
  • where - for places
  • when - for time

Passive Voice

In passive voice, the subject receives the action. Form: be + past participle

Tense Active Passive
Present Simple People recycle plastic bottles. Plastic bottles are recycled.
Past Simple Workers wove natural fibers. Natural fibers were woven.
Future (will) Factories will reduce waste. Waste will be reduced.
Modal (must) Factories must reduce waste. Waste must be reduced.

Comparative & Superlative Forms

Adjective Type Comparative Superlative
One syllable softer softest
One syllable: CVC bigger biggest
Two syllables ending in -y happier happiest
Two+ syllables more expensive most expensive
Irregular better, worse best, worst

So, Such, Enough

so + adjective/adverb Eco-fleece is so warm that it's suitable for winter.
so + many/much/few/little + noun There are so many eco-friendly materials available now.
such + (a/an) + adjective + noun Organic cotton is such a popular material.
adjective/adverb + enough This jacket is not thick enough for cold weather.
enough + noun He doesn't have enough money to buy expensive clothes.
